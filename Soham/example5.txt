

Meera loved reading books more than anything else. Every weekend, she visited the town library to borrow novels and storybooks. The library was an old building filled with wooden shelves, large windows, and thousands of books. It was always peaceful and quiet.

One Saturday afternoon, Meera noticed that the librarian looked worried. Several rare books had disappeared from the history section during the past month. Nobody knew who was taking them. The librarian feared that valuable books might continue disappearing if the thief was not caught soon.

Meera became curious and decided to observe carefully. She noticed that a man wearing a long brown coat visited the library almost every evening. He spent a lot of time near the history shelves but rarely borrowed any books officially. His behavior seemed suspicious to her.

The next day, Meera arrived early and sat near the history section pretending to read a novel. After some time, the same man entered quietly and looked around several times. When the librarian became busy helping another visitor, the man quickly placed a book inside his bag.

Meera immediately informed the librarian in a calm manner. The librarian stopped the man before he could leave the building. After checking the bag, they found several missing books hidden inside it. The man admitted that he planned to sell the rare books for money.

The librarian thanked Meera for her intelligence and alertness. The police were informed, and the stolen books were returned safely to the shelves. News about Meera’s bravery spread across the town, and many people appreciated her quick thinking.

After the incident, the library introduced better security measures to protect valuable books. Meera felt proud that she had helped save an important part of the town’s knowledge and history. The experience taught her that responsibility and observation are very important qualities in life.
